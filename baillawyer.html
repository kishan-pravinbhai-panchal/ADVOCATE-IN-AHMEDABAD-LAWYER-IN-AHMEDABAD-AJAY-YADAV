    What is Anticipatory Bail?

    Anticipatory bail, unlike regular bail granted after arrest, is a preventive measure outlined in Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC). It allows you to file a petition in court seeking bail even before you are formally arrested. The court can grant anticipatory bail under certain conditions, ensuring your liberty if an arrest is imminent.

    When to Seek Anticipatory Bail?

    There are several situations where seeking anticipatory bail might be prudent:

    • FIR Filed Against You: If a First Information Report (FIR) has been lodged against you, even without an arrest, it indicates potential trouble.
    • Likelihood of Arrest: If you have reason to believe you might be arrested due to an investigation or accusation, seeking anticipatory bail can safeguard your freedom.
    • False Implication: If you believe you are being falsely implicated in a crime, anticipatory bail can protect you from wrongful arrest.
