    Court Marriage in Gujarat: A Simplified Union

    A court marriage, solemnised under the Special Marriage Act, 1954, provides a legal framework for couples to get married irrespective of their religion or caste. This eliminates the need for religious ceremonies and simplifies the legal formalities.

    How Advocate Ajay Vadhiya Can Assist You:

    Advocate Vadhiya will provide comprehensive legal guidance throughout your court marriage process in Ahmedabad:

    • Drafting and Filing Notice: Advocate Vadhiya will meticulously draft and file the Notice of Intended Marriage with the Marriage Registrar's office in the district where either partner resides.
    • Objection Handling: If any objections arise during the mandatory waiting period, Advocate Vadhiya will represent you effectively and address any concerns.
    • Appearance in Court: He will accompany you to the court on the designated date and ensure the marriage ceremony is conducted smoothly following legal procedures.
    • Marriage Certificate: Advocate Vadhiya will assist you in obtaining the official marriage certificate, a crucial document for legal recognition of your union.

    Procedure of Court Marriage in Gujarat:

    • Notice of Intended Marriage: Both partners must file a joint Notice of Intended Marriage with the Marriage Registrar's office. Advocate Vadhiya will guide you through this process.
    • Waiting Period:A mandatory waiting period of 30 days exists after filing the notice to allow for any objections.
    • Court Appearance:On the designated date, both partners and at least three witnesses must appear before the Marriage Officer (usually a Magistrate) for the marriage ceremony.
    • Marriage Ceremony: The Marriage Officer will solemnise the marriage, following the legal procedures outlined in the Special Marriage Act.
    • Marriage Certificate: After the ceremony, a marriage certificate will be issued, which is official proof of your legal union.

    Required Documents for Court Marriage Registration:

    • Duly filled Notice of Intended Marriage form (Advocate Vadhiya can help you with this)
    • Proof of residence for both partners (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.)
    • Age proof documents (birth certificate, school leaving certificate, etc.)
    • Passport-sized photographs of both partners
    • Four witnesses with valid ID proofs (if required by the Marriage Officer)
