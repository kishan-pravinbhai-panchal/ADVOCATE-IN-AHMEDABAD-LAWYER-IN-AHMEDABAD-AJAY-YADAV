    Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015:

    This act outlines the legal framework for the care, protection, and rehabilitation of children in need of care and protection, as well as those in conflict with the law.

    Special Juvenile Police Units:

    The act mandates the establishment of Special Juvenile Police Units to handle cases involving children.

    Child Welfare Committees (CWCs):

    CWCs are responsible for ensuring the care and protection of children in need, as well as making decisions about their rehabilitation and adoption.

    Juvenile Justice Boards (JJBs):

    JJBs are responsible for adjudicating cases involving children in conflict with the law, ensuring their rights and rehabilitation.

    Adoption:

    The act governs adoption procedures, making it more child-centric and providing safeguards for adopted children.

    Rehabilitation:

    The law emphasizes the importance of rehabilitating children in conflict with the law through various programs and services.

    Prohibition of Child Labor:

    The Child Labor (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986, sets regulations to prevent and regulate child labor. Link

    Education:

    The Right to Education Act, 2009, guarantees free and compulsory education for children aged 6 to 14. Link

    Legal Services for Differently Abled Children Scheme, 2021:

    The Scheme will help the mentally and physically disabled children including children who are from poor and needy families that cannot afford the education of their child. The recipients of legal literacy would, however, include teachers, doctors and Officers responsible for the welfare of children and other stakeholders. Link

    Anganwadi services:

    It is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme implemented by States/UTs providing a package of six services, namely, (i) Supplementary Nutrition; (ii) Pre-school Non-formal Education; (iii) Nutrition & Health Education; (iv) Immunization; (v) Health Check-up; and (vi) Referral Services has been provided to all eligible beneficiaries, namely, children in the age group of 0-6 years, pregnant women and lactating mothers through the platform of Anganwadi Centres across the country. Link
